Career to Date

The stepping stones that have brought me to where I am today

Ackcio, Software Engineer

(Apr - Dec 2020)

Ackcio is a startup that provides industrial remote monitoring solutions; low-powered robust networking hardware that withstand harsh weather conditions. I worked on the client-facing .NET Core application that runs on a RasPi, initiating critical data visualization features to help clients optimize performance by identifying the path taken by packets in the network. Limited to the Pi's computing power and a 3.6v battery, I honed my skills in optimizing software performance down to energy consumption.

Boxed (New York), Software Engineer

January-March 2020

At Boxed, my team worked on developing an ad platform, which would help advertisers on our platform place, bid for, and manage advertisements for their campaigns. My internship was unfortunately cut short by the pandemic, but I had learnt a great deal in the 3 months. As a fullstack developer, this internship gave me the opportunity to develop beyond my inclinations towards frontend work. Having the flexibility to insert myself at various parts of the development process (designing interface, implementing api calls, data migrations, etc.) was a welcome challenge.

Projects were developed using React-Apollo-GraphQL stack, with Postgres db using the Hasura console, and Docker.

Waffle, UI Design Intern

December 2019

The diversification of food delivery applications, mobile, and card payments managed by different payment gateways had left cashiers to use different consoles to process payment. Moreover, the POS systems widely used in Singapore are clunky and costly, require manual backups, and hardly have optimized UI. The Waffle startup aims to modernize the POS systems into an integrated system that provides a great user experience for cashiers and managers, by integrating various payment gateways, sales data analytics, and flexible menu updates, to an iOS application.

Working with an early-stage company let me develop my UI skills by translating requirements into the application design, in quick and successive iterations closely following client interviews.

Tetsuyu Healthcare

Summer 2019

Tetsuyu Healthcare is a health-tech startup that aids home caregivers and nurses through their software suite. Here, I initiated the modernization of their company website by completely redesigning it in accordance with Tetsuyu's image, and built with ReactJS. This included coordinating and executing the shoots for the website's media content, professionally finished with editing softwares.

In addition, I led the interns' team in the collaboration with A*STAR on processing data to train the machine learning imaging model to recognize wound tissues as part of Cares4Wounds technology.
